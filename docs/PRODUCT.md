# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Engineering PhD students and self-directed researchers working through MAE-6291 (Generative AI for Engineering Research) on their own, after the live course ended. They arrive curious but time-poor, often on a laptop next to their actual research work. The job to be done: absorb each session's notes/slides/videos, check their understanding, and track progress through 14 lessons.

## Product Purpose

An interactive, self-paced edition of Prof. Lorena Barba's open course materials (CC BY 4.0). It exists so the one-semester seminar outlives its semester: embedded viewers keep learners in flow instead of bouncing across Google Docs/Slides/YouTube tabs, and grounded quizzes turn passive reading into checked understanding. Success = a learner finishes lessons and can answer the quizzes, not just skim.

## Brand Personality

Scholarly, warm, confident. The feeling of a beautifully typeset university press book that happens to be interactive — academic gravitas without stuffiness, warmth without cuteness. It should evoke calm focus and quiet credibility, honoring the source course's serif-led identity (Merriweather, Barba group).

## Anti-references

- Gamified ed-tech (Duolingo-style streaks, mascots, confetti) — this is a graduate seminar, not a game.
- Generic SaaS dashboard chrome — gray cards, cookie-cutter shadows, default Tailwind blue.
- The previous iteration's neon-violet-on-near-black "AI product" look — dark gradients and purple/pink accents read as hype, not scholarship.
- Cramped documentation sites that treat reading as an afterthought.

## Design Principles

1. **The page is a reading instrument.** Typography and measure come first; chrome recedes. Embedded third-party content (white Docs/PDF frames) must sit harmoniously, not glare.
2. **Warmth through material, not decoration.** Paper-toned surfaces, ink-toned text, one confident accent — no gradients doing the work personality should.
3. **Progress is quiet pride.** Completion states feel like a librarian's neat checkmark, not a slot machine.
4. **Honor the source.** This is Barba's course; the design frames her material with the care of a good book designer, never upstaging it.
5. **Every state is designed.** Light and dark are both first-class; quizzes have considered correct/incorrect states; focus rings and reduced motion are part of the design, not patches.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA: body text ≥4.5:1 against its background in both themes; large text ≥3:1; visible focus states on all interactive elements; `prefers-reduced-motion` respected; theme toggle persists and defaults to the OS preference. Quiz correctness is never conveyed by color alone (icons + text labels accompany green/red).
